About Tetralogy of Fallot

Understanding the most common cyanotic congenital heart defect

What is Tetralogy of Fallot?

Tetralogy of Fallot (ToF) is a congenital (present at birth) heart condition that affects approximately 1 in every 2,518 babies born in the United States. Named after French physician Etienne-Louis Arthur Fallot, who first described it in 1888, ToF is the most common cyanotic congenital heart defect.

The condition involves four structural abnormalities of the heart that occur together, reducing the amount of oxygen-rich blood that reaches the body. Without treatment, babies with ToF may appear blue (cyanotic) due to low oxygen levels in their blood.

The Four Defects

Click on any defect card below to learn more details.

1

Ventricular Septal Defect (VSD)

A hole in the wall (septum) between the two lower chambers of the heart (ventricles), allowing oxygen-rich and oxygen-poor blood to mix.

2

Pulmonary Stenosis

Narrowing of the pulmonary valve and the passage from the right ventricle to the pulmonary artery, reducing blood flow to the lungs.

3

Right Ventricular Hypertrophy

The muscle of the right ventricle becomes thickened because it has to work harder than normal to pump blood through the narrowed pulmonary valve.

4

Overriding Aorta

The aorta is positioned directly over the ventricular septal defect instead of over the left ventricle, receiving blood from both ventricles.

Diagnosis & Treatment

Diagnosis

  • Prenatal ultrasound (fetal echocardiography)
  • Postnatal echocardiogram
  • Pulse oximetry screening
  • Cardiac catheterization
  • Chest X-ray and ECG

Surgical Repair

  • Complete intracardiac repair (usually within first year)
  • Temporary shunt surgery (for very small or premature babies)
  • Pulmonary valve replacement (later in life if needed)
  • Regular follow-up with a cardiologist throughout life
  • 95%+ survival rate with modern surgical techniques

Our Mission

At Tetralogy of Fallot Lifeline, we believe that every family facing a ToF diagnosis deserves access to clear, compassionate, and accurate information. Our mission is to:

  • Provide accessible, evidence-based medical education about Tetralogy of Fallot
  • Connect families with support networks and trusted resources
  • Share inspiring stories from Heart Warriors and their families
  • Advocate for increased awareness and research funding for congenital heart defects
  • Reduce the fear and isolation that comes with a CHD diagnosis
